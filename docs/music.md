# Loose Cannon — Music AI Generation Prompts

Prompts for a **music AI generator** (Suno, Udio, AIVA, Stable Audio, etc.) to produce tracks for **Loose Cannon**: a browser isometric crime MMO blending *Syndicate* street tactics, *Cannon Fodder* dark crew comedy, and *Kingpin* foul-mouthed gangster fantasy.

## Product tone (keep every track on-brand)

| Do | Don’t |
|----|--------|
| 1990s–00s crime movie grit, neon rain, diesel streets | Clean cyberpunk / CHIP / synthwave “future corp” |
| Dark humor under the tension (not clown music) | Cartoon slapstick score |
| Gritty bass, dirty guitars, analog grit, sparse brass | Over-polished EDM drops only |
| Named-goon pathos under the swagger | Heroic Hollywood superhero theme |
| **Instrumental unless noted** (no lyrics needed for in-game beds) | Long vocal verses that fight VO / SFX |

**Visual world:** rain-slick isometric city, bars, pawn shops, war zone south of the tracks, dumpsters, memorial walls, cheap pistols and big mouths.

**Technical tips for generators**

- Prefer **instrumental**, **loop-friendly** where noted (seamless or near-seamless ends).
- Target **~90–140 BPM** unless a cue says otherwise.
- Export **stereo MP3 or WAV**; game beds play **quietly under SFX** (~music at ~0.1–0.15 vs louder combat sounds).
- Generate **2–3 variants** of each bed; keep the best loop.

## 1. Title / main menu / splash

**Use:** Login screen, splash art, “Hit the Streets” energy before join.

**Prompt A — primary title**

> Instrumental crime theme for a 1990s gangster isometric video game. Dark neon rain city at night, gritty boom-bap meets dirty electric guitar and low brass stabs. Confident, dangerous, slightly tongue-in-cheek swagger — not superheroic. Mid-tempo ~100 BPM, strong bassline, sparse hi-hats, cinematic but street-level. No vocals. 90–120 seconds with a memorable opening hook and a cold ending that can cut to silence.

**Prompt B — alternate (more Kingpin)**

> Instrumental title theme for an adult crime game like Kingpin meets Syndicate. Dieselpunk grit, industrial drums, growling bass, occasional saxophone or muted trumpet over distorted guitar. Rainy docks and neon pawn shops. Menacing but fun, 95–105 BPM, no lyrics. Opening that feels like a crime movie logo sting expanding into a full groove.

**Prompt C — short logo sting (5–12 s)**

> Ultra-short instrumental logo sting for a crime video game: two hits of dirty bass + brass, then a neon guitar scrape. Dark humor energy. No melody loop, just a punchy 6–10 second brand hit for the splash screen.

## 2. Hub / safe downtown / exploration (ambient)

**Use:** Walking Skidrow, bars (Rusty Nail), shops, recruiting, free roam outdoors outside combat.

**Prompt A — downtown night bed (loop)**

> Seamless looping instrumental ambient exploration music for an isometric crime game hub. Rainy neon downtown, low-key boom-bap or trip-hop beat under atmospheric pads and distant traffic. Sparse electric piano or muted guitar licks. Cool, watchful, slightly sleazy bar energy — not combat. ~85–95 BPM, instrumental only, soft dynamics so dialogue and SFX stay on top. 2–3 minute loopable bed.

**Prompt B — bar interior**

> Instrumental lounge-grit underscore for a seedy crime-game bar called The Rusty Nail. Sticky wood, neon beer signs, jukebox far in the corner. Slow funky bass, brushed drums or soft hip-hop pocket, occasional smoky guitar. Intimate, humorous tension, no vocals. ~80–90 BPM, suitable for looping under NPC dialogue.

**Prompt C — day cycle / warmer daylight**

> Instrumental daytime exploration music for a grimy crime city in an isometric game. Slightly brighter than night version: dusty funk guitar, lazy drums, warm bass, still dangerous under the surface. Not cheerful pop. ~90 BPM, seamless loop feel, instrumental only.

**Prompt D — Titty Twister / club (optional)**

> Instrumental 18+ gentlemen’s club underscore for a crime game (suggestive, not explicit). Slow pulse, bass-heavy, neon pink/purple synth accents, restrained disco or R&B instrumental groove. Sleazy cool, never comedic “wah-wah” parody. ~90–100 BPM, loopable, low-key enough for dialogue.

## 3. Tension / heat rising / war fringe

**Use:** Crossing into war zone, high heat, stalking a job objective, “about to go loud.”

**Prompt**

> Instrumental tension underscore for an isometric crime game when the player enters a hostile district. Heartbeat bass, ticking percussion, distant sirens texture (synthetic, not sample-pack cliché), rising dissonant pads. Builds anxiety without full combat drums. ~100–110 BPM, instrumental, 90–150 seconds, can fade into action music.

## 4. Combat / action / posse firefight

**Use:** Street fights, warehouse wipe, gunfights, chase-and-shoot.

**Prompt A — main combat loop**

> Instrumental combat music for an isometric squad crime game (Syndicate tactics + gangster attitude). Driving drums, aggressive bass, staccato guitar or industrial hits, sharp brass accents. Fast enough for gunfights (~120–130 BPM) but not chaotic metal. Dark fun, not horror. Seamless loop friendly, no vocals, high energy but leaves headroom for gun SFX.

**Prompt B — heavier boss / raid**

> Instrumental boss-fight style combat for a crime game warehouse raid. Heavier kick and distorted bass, machine-gun hi-hat patterns, occasional cinematic hits. Menacing and kinetic, ~125 BPM, instrumental, loopable 2 minutes. Feels like clearing a room with a named posse of goons.

**Prompt C — short combat sting (in/out)**

> Short instrumental combat sting 8–15 seconds: sudden drums + bass drop for “fight starts,” and a mirror 8-second “fight ends / last man down” decay into silence. Crime game, gritty, no melody vocals.

## 6. Victory / mission complete / payday

**Use:** Job complete notify, extract success, “+$cash +rep.”

**Prompt A — full victory cue**

> Instrumental victory / mission-complete cue for a darkly comic crime game. Triumphant but cheap and streetwise — like getting paid in a back alley, not saving the world. Short brass or guitar hook over a confident beat, then a cool-down into silence. 20–40 seconds, no lyrics. Cannon Fodder “we won (and someone might still be dead)” irony optional as slight melancholy under the swagger.

**Prompt B — short UI jingle**

> Ultra-short instrumental cash-register / payday jingle for a crime game UI toast: 3–6 seconds, punchy bass + metallic hit + cheeky minor-key resolution. No vocals.

## 7. Defeat / wipe / “you’re down”

**Use:** Player death, full posse wipe, mission failed.

**Prompt A — wipe**

> Instrumental death / wipe sting for a crime game with dark humor. Heavy thud, descending bass, brief funeral-march joke (subtle), not sad orchestral weepiness. 10–20 seconds. Feels like “everyone’s dead, time to hire new meat.”

**Prompt B — mission failed**

> Short instrumental failure sting for a crime job gone wrong. Sour brass or bent guitar, cut drums, awkward silence. 8–12 seconds, adult crime comedy tone.
